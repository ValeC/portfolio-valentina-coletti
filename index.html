User research

Description

In order to redesign an existing system or to design a new one that meets the needs of the end-users, it is important to understand the latter, their tasks and the environment where the system is or will be used. It may appear a simple task, but actually there are many aspects that should be explored.
Firstly, data regarding the end-users themselves such as biography, professional status, health, relation to technology and so on.
Secondly, their tasks and environment.
All these aspects will bring to light the needs of the end-users.

Thus, at the end of this research, the elements to consider for the system requirements should be established.

In the UCD process, user research focuses on these goals by applying a variety of methods such as: interview, survey, observation, persona, scenario, storyboards and so on.

Prototyping

Description

During this phase, some prototypes of user interfaces are designed based on requirements previously established during the user research phase.

Firstly, they will be drafted on paper with few details (low-fidelity prototype). This allows an easy and cheap way of exploring first ideas from which more detailed prototypes will mature through iterative process of evaluation and redesign.

Secondly, once the design objectives are mostly achieved, a more detailed computer-based prototype (high-fidelity prototype) can be created. This allows a simulation of the interaction between the end-user and the interface (when clicking on a user interface element, a fictive command is executed), which conveys a more realistic idea of the final computer system.

The process of designing interfaces should be based on design guidelines. The most common are visibility, feedback, constraints, mapping, consistency and affordance. Producing prototypes of the target system allows making changes before actually implementing it. Changes accomplished after the system is coded have a higher cost in terms of time and money. Furthermore, involving typical end-users in this phase is really important because designers are not the real end-users and may design prototypes that do not meet the users' needs.

User Evaluation

Description

The third phase consists in evaluating if the prototype, which has been created in the previous phase, is usable: effective, efficient and satisfactory to use. The most commonly performed kind of evaluation is the one that involves the end-users (usability evaluation). Typically, they are supposed to accomplish some tasks with the prototype, to give feedback about problems encountered and to explain their expectations. This phase helps designers understand what should be improved.
At the end of this phase, designers should have gathered data in several formats such as comments, notes, audio/video/screen recordings.
